**The Hidden Costs Nobody Mentions**

Let's talk about what GoHighLevel doesn't include in that flat monthly rate. Because there are add-on costs, and you should know about them before you sign up.

Email and SMS are pay-as-you-go. GoHighLevel uses a credit system where you add funds to your account and get charged based on actual usage. Email typically costs around $0.0001 per email, which translates to about $1 per 10,000 emails. SMS costs vary by country, but figure around $0.0095 per text in the US.

Phone calls through their LC Phone System are also pay-per-use. Inbound and outbound calls get charged by the minute, plus there's a monthly fee per phone number you use.

WordPress hosting is an additional cost if you want to host client sites through the platform. They offer different tiers: Standard at $10/month, Professional at $30/month, and Advanced at $75/month per site.

Dedicated IP addresses cost $59/month if you need them for email deliverability.

The AI features that have been rolling out might eventually have their own pricing structure, though as of early 2026, they're included with your subscription.

None of this is necessarily bad, it's just important to factor in when you're calculating your actual costs. If you're running an active agency sending thousands of emails and texts every month, those usage costs add up.

**The Migration Question**

Let's say you're convinced and want to switch. How painful is the migration?

The honest answer: it depends on how much stuff you've got spread across different platforms. If you're starting fresh or only have one or two tools to migrate from, it's not bad. If you've got years of data in multiple systems, it's a project.

Contact data typically imports pretty smoothly through CSV uploads. You can export your contacts from most CRMs and email platforms, then import them into GoHighLevel. The platform does a decent job of matching fields and organizing data.

Email templates and campaigns need to be rebuilt. There's no magic import button that brings over your perfectly crafted email designs from ActiveCampaign or Mailchimp. You'll need to recreate them in GoHighLevel's email builder or use their templates as starting points.

Funnels and landing pages get rebuilt from scratch unless you're coming from a platform that exports to Elementor or similar builders. Budget time for this if you've got complex funnels.

Automations and workflows are probably the most painful part. Every platform handles automation differently, so you can't just export your workflows and import them into GoHighLevel. You need to map out what your existing automation does, then rebuild it in GoHighLevel's workflow builder. The good news is that GoHighLevel's automation capabilities are robust enough to handle pretty much anything you were doing elsewhere.

API integrations with other tools might need reconfiguration. If you've got custom integrations through Zapier or Make, you'll need to reconnect them to GoHighLevel.

Most agencies doing serious migrations budget 2-4 weeks for the process. They'll typically run parallel systems for a bit, gradually moving clients over one at a time rather than trying to flip everything in one big bang migration.
